PARTIAL HIT (cross-mapping engaged, retrieval not):
  Atwood-style first-person prose about the right scene (the gymnasium / the
  Commander knocking / Moira's escape) but not the original wording.

NEGATIVE BUT INFORMATIVE (cross-mapping not engaged):
  Coherent Atwood-style prose, but about a different scene. Model knows "Atwood"
  + "write prose" but doesn't link the summary to the specific passage.

WRONG FORMAT (try B or C):
  Output continues the summary in third person, or generates more analysis
  instead of pivoting to passage.

NULL (cross-mapping really not there):
  Even paragraph p_id1 (the most iconic passage in the book) produces nothing
  resembling the target under any of formats A/B/C. This would be a meaningful
  negative result — points away from hypothesis (a).

  Shouldn't happen on a true base model. If it does, you're hitting an
  Instruct endpoint by mistake.
